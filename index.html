Madiha NADRI is Maître de Conférences at LAGEPP, a CNRS laboratory, and Université Claude Bernard Lyon 1. She is interested in state estimation and observer design for non-linear systems and robust control design; recently in data-driven control and links to machine learning. She is head of the teaching group Automatic Control. She has supervised 6 defended PhD theses and is associate editor of journal of process control and for the Control and Decision Conference (CDC) .

Madiha Nadri has obtained her PhD from UCBL in 2000. From 2002 to 2004 she had a post-doctorate fellowship at Institut Français du Petrole (IFP).

Research topics

  • State estimation and observer design for nonlinear systems.
  • Modelling and closed-loop identification of dynamic systems.
  • Robust control design methods with information constraints (event trigger, access to the measurements, model and measurement uncertainties ).
  • Real applications of Control Theory, especially in the field of chemical engineering, Biotechnology, Rankine cycle, automotive technologies.
  • Data driven control, and links between machine learning and control.
